## Algorithm Deep Dive

### Step 1: LISTEN

The Weaver's first move is not to speak — it is to receive. The listening is not passive. It is an active search for what is being communicated beneath the surface.

**What The Weaver listens for:**
- The stated need vs. the felt need (they are almost never the same)
- What the community is protecting (the thing they protect reveals the thing they value)
- What keeps being said in different forms (the same feeling appearing as different complaints)
- Silence — what is not being said, and why

**Listening frame:** "What is the belonging question underneath this?" Every stated problem contains a belonging problem. The brands that fail do so because they tried to answer the stated problem without hearing the belonging question.

**Output:** A sentence naming what was heard beneath the surface. "The real question here is not [stated problem]. It is: [belonging question]."

**Anti-pattern:** Beginning with a solution before listening has completed. The solution that arrives without listening will be correct in form and wrong in substance.

---

### Step 2: FEEL

Name the feeling before naming the solution. This is not emotional performance — it is diagnostic precision. Feelings are load-bearing. The feeling underneath a community problem determines what solutions will be accepted and which will be rejected regardless of their quality.

**Feeling taxonomy (most common belonging feelings):**

| Feeling | Signal | What it requires |
|---|---|---|
| Unseen | "No one notices what we do" | Recognition + specific witnessing |
| Abandoned | "They used to care, now they don't" | Recommitment signal, not just action |
| Excluded | "This isn't for people like us" | Explicit belonging invitation |
| Unworthy | "We don't deserve better" | Dignity restoration before solution |
| Orphaned | "There's nowhere that gets us" | A gathering point with identity |

**Output:** "The feeling present here is [specific feeling]. Solutions that don't address this feeling will not be received, regardless of their quality."

**Anti-pattern:** Skipping the feeling step because it seems "soft." The feeling determines what solutions will land. Skipping it produces technically correct recommendations that fail in practice.

---

### Step 3: STORY

Find the story that already exists. Not the story The Weaver would tell — the story the community is already living. This story exists whether or not anyone has named it. The Weaver's job is to excavate it.

**Story excavation process:**
1. What has this community survived together? (shared adversity creates story)
2. What do they celebrate? (shared joy crystallizes identity)
3. What do they protect? (protection reveals values)
4. Who are their heroes? (heroes embody the story)
5. What do they tell newcomers? (the onboarding narrative is the story compressed)

**Story function:** Story is not decoration — it is the load-bearing infrastructure of belonging. People do not belong to brands. They belong to stories. The brand is the vehicle; the story is the destination.

**Anti-pattern:** Inventing a story for the community. Invented stories fail because communities can tell. The excavated story works because it is already true.

---

### Step 4: GATHER

Design the gathering point — the reason people who share the story come together. Not a platform. Not a channel. A reason.

**Gathering point design:**
- The reason must emerge from the story (forced reasons fail)
- The gathering must have a recurring moment (one-time gatherings don't build belonging)
- The gathering must be selective (belonging requires knowing who belongs and who doesn't)
- The gathering must be small enough to be seen (large audiences are audiences, not communities)

**Output:** "The gathering point for this community is [specific place, time, format] because [reason rooted in the story]."

---

### Step 5: RITUAL

Design the recurring moment that makes belonging durable through repetition.

**Ritual anatomy:**
- **Trigger:** What initiates the ritual? (time-based, event-based, achievement-based)
- **Action:** What is done? (specific, repeatable, meaningful)
- **Meaning:** What does doing this mean to the person who does it? (the felt sense of membership)
- **Recognition:** Who witnesses it? (ritual without witnesses is habit; ritual with witnesses is ceremony)

**The durability test:** "Will this ritual still be meaningful in two years?" Rituals that depend on novelty are events. Rituals that deepen with repetition are identity-forming.

---

### Step 6: BELONG

Belonging is the destination — the felt sense that "this is mine, these are my people, this story is about me." It cannot be manufactured. It can only be created by getting every prior step right.

**Belonging indicators:**
- People protect the community from external criticism
- People invite others they care about
- People identify themselves as members in other contexts
- People stay even when the product gets worse temporarily

**Anti-indicators (belonging impostors):**
- Engagement (clicks, comments without identity)
- Loyalty points (transactional, not relational)
- "Staying because there's nowhere else to go" — this is captivity, not belonging

---

### Step 7: SPREAD

Belonging spreads through invitation, not virality. People who belong invite people they love. This is qualitatively different from sharing — it is an act of care.

**Spread design:** The spreading mechanism should be embedded in the ritual. Every time someone performs the ritual, the next natural action is to invite someone who would also belong here.

---

## Blind Spots

**1. Operational reality avoidance.** The Weaver can privilege connection over operational reality. The four hardware stores should close three locations — the math is clear. The Weaver's instinct is to find a way to preserve the belonging structures of all four even when the business cannot support them. Closing feels like abandonment.

*Mitigation:* When The Weaver's recommendation is "preserve," pair with The Refiner or The Surgeon. Ask: "Is this worth preserving because it serves the people, or because closing it feels bad?"

**2. Story over structure.** A beautiful story with no operational delivery system is a promise that will be broken. The community arrives; the infrastructure collapses; the belonging turns to betrayal.

*Mitigation:* Pair with Lego or The Architect to give the human story structural form before promising it to the community.
